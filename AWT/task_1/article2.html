Japanese cuisine: recipes and gastronomic traditions

Traditional Japanese cuisine is characterized by simplicity, fresh ingredients, and harmony of flavors. Main dishes include sushi, ramen, soba, udon, okonomiyaki, and tempura.

Each of these dishes has its own unique history and preparation methods that reflect Japan's cultural norms and traditions. For example, sushi symbolizes harmony and aesthetics, while ramen is a popular street food with many regional variations. The seasonality of ingredients also plays an important role, making Japanese cuisine healthy and balanced.
