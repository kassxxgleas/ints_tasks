Traditional Japanese festivals and their significance

Holidays play an important role in Japanese culture, connecting centuries-old traditions with modern times. They reflect the values, beliefs, and way of life of the Japanese people, making the culture vibrant and diverse.

Setsubun — sviatok začiatku jari

Setsubun is celebrated on February 3 and symbolizes the beginning of spring according to the lunar calendar. The main tradition is throwing roasted beans out of the house while shouting "Demons out, good luck in!" to drive away evil spirits and attract good fortune. People also eat as many beans as their age, believing it will bring them protection and health.

  1. Discarding roasted beans
  2. Bushes and windows are being cleaned
  3. Prayers for the health of the family are held

Hana Matsuri — Flower Festival

The Buddha's birthday is celebrated on April 8. Throughout the country, temples are decorated with flowers and believers pour sweet tea made from flower petals over statues of Buddha . This holiday symbolizes rebirth and the beauty of nature, combining religious ceremonies with the awakening of spring.
